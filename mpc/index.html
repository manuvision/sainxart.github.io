The boom bap workflow

"I don't sample to be lazy. I sample to have a conversation with the past."

— Inspired by Madlib
How Madlib / Dilla would use the MPC Sample
1
Find your source
Play a vinyl-style track from your phone via USB into the MPC Sample. Jazz, soul, old funk records work best for lofi & boom bap.
2
Sample Record → grab it
Hit SAMPLE RECORD. Set Source to USB. Let it play, hit a pad to start recording. Grab 4–8 bars of something that feels right.
3
Chop it up
In Sample Mode, press CHOP. Use Regions 4 or 8 to auto-slice. Each pad now plays one piece. Play them out of order — that's the art.
4
Tune & filter each slice
Press B2 for Tune. Dial Semi Tune on K1. Press B3 for Filter — add a Classic LPF with some resonance. This gives that warm, dusty MPC sound.
5
Add drums on other pads
Press PAD BANK to switch banks. Load a kick on A1, snare on A2, hi-hat on A3. Use the built-in boom bap kits or record your own.
6
Record a sequence
Press SEQ → SEQ RECORD → PLAY. Tap your drums first (1 bar), then add chops on the next loop. Keep adding. Stop when it feels right.
7
Apply the compressor
SHIFT + PAD 5 (COMPRESSOR). Turn Amount up to ~60%. This "glues" everything together — the signature boom bap punch.
8
Export your beat
SHIFT + PAD 12 (SONG). Add your sequence. Press Export → Audio Mixdown. Save to name. Transfer via USB to your phone.
