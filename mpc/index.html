The boom bap workflow
"I don't sample to be lazy. I sample to have a conversation with the past. Find the moment that moves you, then make it yours."
— Inspired by Madlib & J Dilla
How the greats would use the MPC Sample
1
Find your source
Play jazz, soul, or funk from your phone via USB. Look for chords, horn stabs, or a melodic moment that hits you.
2
Sample Record → grab the moment
SAMPLE RECORD → Source: USB. Wait for the right bar, tap a pad. Capture 4–8 seconds. Stop by tapping the same pad.
3
Chop it into slices
SAMPLE mode → CHOP → Regions 4 or 8. Each pad now plays one piece. Play them out of order. Find combinations that surprise you.
4
Tune and filter each slice
B2 → Semi Tune (K1) to transpose harmonically. B3 → Classic filter, Cutoff 60–75. That's the warm, dusty 90s MPC sound.
5
Load drums on Bank A
PAD BANK → Bank A. Kick → PAD 1, Snare → PAD 2, Hi-hat → PAD 3. Use built-in BoomBap kits or record your own.
6
Record your sequence
SEQ → SEQ RECORD → PLAY. Drums first, then chops on the next loop pass. Overdub keeps layering. Stop = STOP button.
7
Glue it with the compressor
SHIFT + PAD 5. Set Amount to ~60%. Turn on Color for tape warmth. This gives your beat punch and glue.
8
Export your track
SHIFT + PAD 12 (SONG). Insert your sequence. Export → Audio Mixdown. Transfer via USB to your phone. Done.
